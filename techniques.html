Master the Strokes

Swimming efficiency comes from minimizing drag and maximizing propulsion. Explore the mechanics of the four competitive strokes.

Freestyle (Front Crawl)

The fastest and most efficient stroke. Key focuses include high elbow recovery, continuous flutter kick, and neutral head position.

Pro Tip:

Rotate your hips with each stroke to lengthen your reach and engage your core muscles, not just your shoulders.

Butterfly

The most physically demanding stroke. It requires simultaneous arm movement and a dolphin kick. Rhythm is everything.

Pro Tip:

Don't lift your head too high to breathe. Keep your chin close to the water surface to maintain forward momentum.

Backstroke

The only stroke swum on the back. It involves alternating arm movements and a flutter kick, similar to freestyle.

Pro Tip:

Keep your hips high near the surface. If they drop, you create massive drag. Imagine balancing a cup of water on your forehead.

Breaststroke

The oldest stroke. It relies on a frog kick and simultaneous arm pull. It is the slowest but most technical stroke.

Pro Tip:

Shoot your hands forward quickly (the "glide") after each pull. That split second of streamlining is where you gain distance.
